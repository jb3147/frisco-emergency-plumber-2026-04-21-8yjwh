Why Water Heaters Fail

  • Age & Corrosion

    Tanks over 10 years old often develop rust and leaks

  • Sediment Buildup

    Mineral deposits reduce efficiency and cause heating failure

  • Component Failure

    Heating elements, thermostats, and valves wear out

What Is a Water Heater Failure?

A water heater failure occurs when your tank or tankless system stops producing hot water, leaks, makes unusual noises, or shows signs of rust and corrosion. These failures can happen suddenly and leave you without hot water for showers, laundry, and cleaning—creating discomfort and potential property damage if left unaddressed.

Who This Service Is For

  • Homeowners

  • Rental Property Managers

  • Commercial Businesses

  • New Homebuyers

How Our Water Heater Service Works

From your first call to the moment hot water flows again, here is exactly what to expect from Frisco Elite Plumbing.

1

You Call & We Dispatch

When you call Frisco Elite Plumbing with a water heater emergency, our dispatcher listens carefully to your situation, provides immediate guidance (like shutting off the water supply if there is a leak), and dispatches a licensed technician with an estimated arrival time. We prioritize based on urgency and risk.

Typical wait time: 30–60 minutes in Frisco city limits

2

Technician Arrives & Assesses

Our technician inspects your water heater, checks for leaks, tests temperature and pressure, examines the age and condition of components, and reviews your usage patterns. If there is active water, we contain and shut it off safely to prevent further damage.

Assessment typically takes 15–20 minutes

3

Repair or Replace Decision

We explain what went wrong and present your options: repair the failing component (if cost-effective and reliable) or replace the entire unit with a new, energy-efficient model. We provide transparent pricing and help you decide based on age, condition, and long-term value.

Most decisions made within 10 minutes; we can proceed same-day

4

Repair or Installation

Whether repairing or replacing, our technician works efficiently to restore your hot water system. For replacements, we handle removal of the old unit, installation of the new one, connection of gas or electric lines, and pressure testing to ensure everything is safe and code-compliant.

Repair: 30–60 minutes | Replacement: 1–2 hours

5

Testing, Cleanup & Documentation

We test your hot water at multiple fixtures, verify temperature settings, clean up the work area, and provide you with documentation of the service (including photos if damage occurred). We also explain warranty coverage and any maintenance tips to extend the life of your new or repaired system.

Final walkthrough and explanation: 10–15 minutes
